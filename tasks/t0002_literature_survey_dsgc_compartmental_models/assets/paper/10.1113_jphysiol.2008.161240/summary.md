# Physiological Properties of Direction-Selective Ganglion Cells in Early Postnatal and Adult Mouse Retina

* **Authors**: Minggang Chen, Shijun Weng, Qiudong Deng, Zhen Xu, Shigang He (all from Chinese

* **DOI**: `10.1113/jphysiol.2008.161240`

## Abstract

Selective responses of retinal ganglion cells (RGCs) to the direction of motion have been recorded
extracellularly from the rabbit and the mouse retina at eye opening. Recently, it has been shown
that the development of this circuitry is light independent. Using whole-cell patch clamp recording,
we report here that mouse early postnatal direction-selective ganglion cells (DSGCs) showed lower
membrane excitability, lower reliability of synaptic transmission and much slower kinetics of light
responses compared with adult DSGCs. However, the degree of direction selectivity of early postnatal
DSGCs measured by the direction-selective index and the width of the directional tuning curve was
almost identical to that of adult DSGCs. The DSGCs exhibited a clear selectivity for the direction
of motion at the onset of light sensitivity. Furthermore, the degree of direction selectivity was
not affected by rearing in complete darkness from birth to postnatal day 11 or 30. The formation of
the retinal neurocircuitry for coding motion direction is completely independent of light.

## Overview

This paper characterises the electrophysiological properties of ON-OFF direction-selective retinal
ganglion cells (DSGCs) across postnatal development in the C57BL/6N mouse, comparing cells at
eye-opening age (P11-P13) with adult cells (>P30). The central question is whether the functional
computations underlying direction selectivity mature at the same rate as basic excitability and
synaptic properties.

The authors find a striking dissociation: early postnatal DSGCs are markedly less excitable, have
weaker and less reliable synaptic inputs, and display much slower EPSCs than adult cells, yet their
directional tuning -- quantified by the direction-selective index (DSI) and the half-width of the
tuning curve -- is indistinguishable from adults. Direction-selective responses emerge as early as
P11, three days before eye opening, coinciding with the earliest light-evoked responses.

A second key finding is the light-independence of circuit formation. Mice dark-reared from birth to
P11 or P30 show no change in DSI or tuning-curve width compared to normally reared littermates,
ruling out experience-dependent refinement and supporting a genetically pre-specified wiring
programme.

The methodological core is whole-cell patch-clamp in the isolated whole-mount retina, combining
loose-patch identification, voltage-clamp EPSC recordings, and current-clamp current-injection
protocols. Cells were confirmed as bistratified ON-OFF DSGCs by intracellular dye fills.

## Architecture, Models and Methods

**Preparation.** Whole-mount retinae from C57BL/6N mice at P11-P13, P18, and >P30 were maintained at
32-35 degrees C in oxygenated Ames medium. Dark-reared cohorts were raised in complete darkness from
P0 to P11 or P0 to P30.

**Cell identification.** ON-OFF DSGCs identified in loose-patch mode (pipette 2-4 MOhm, Ames medium)
using flashing spots and moving bars to map receptive fields and confirm direction selectivity. Only
cells with DSI > 0.3 were retained. Morphological confirmation by intracellular Neurobiotin/Lucifer
Yellow fills verified bistratified dendritic arbors post hoc.

**Direction-selective index.** DSI = (R_pref - R_null) / (R_pref + R_null). Responses are spike
counts to a 100 um x 500 um bar moving 1500 um at approximately 750 um/s in each of 12 directions
spaced 30 degrees apart.

**Tuning curve width.** Half-width at half-maximum of the polar response curve.

**Voltage clamp.** Pipette 4-7 MOhm; intracellular: caesium methanesulphonate 120 mM, CsCl 0.5 mM,
EGTA 5 mM, HEPES 10 mM, ATP 4 mM, GTP 0.5 mM, QX-314 5 mM. EPSCs at -65 mV and 0 mV to separate
excitatory and inhibitory components. EPSC rising/falling phase kinetics compared between P11-P13
and adult cells.

**Current clamp.** Intracellular: potassium gluconate 120 mM, NaCl 5 mM, KCl 10 mM, MgCl2 1 mM, EGTA
1 mM, HEPES 10 mM, ATP 2 mM, GTP 0.5 mM. Current steps of varying amplitude injected to assess
membrane excitability across age groups.

**Repeated-flash protocol.** Two identical flashes delivered in succession (R1, R2). R2/R1 ratio
quantifies synaptic reliability.

**Data acquisition.** Low-pass filtered at 2 kHz, digitised with Digidata 1320A (Axon Instruments).
Seal resistance >1 GOhm required; liquid junction potential of 10 mV always corrected.

## Results

* P11 DSGCs fired **21.2 +/- 4.4 spikes** (ON) and **27.0 +/- 3.1 spikes** (OFF) per stationary
  flash vs **42.9 +/- 5.1** (ON) and **40.5 +/- 4.3** (OFF) in adults (*p* < 0.05)
* Peak firing rate at P11: **95.9 +/- 16.5 Hz** (ON) and **107.5 +/- 13.5 Hz** (OFF), roughly half
  adult values of **166.4 +/- 14.5 Hz** (ON) and **189.7 +/- 17.1 Hz** (OFF)
* DSI of early postnatal cells was **not significantly different** from adults (*p* > 0.05) despite
  approximately 2-fold differences in spike count and firing rate
* Tuning-curve half-width was **not significantly different** across P11, P13, P18, and adult groups
  (*p* > 0.05)
* P11 cells entered depolarisation block at current > 100 pA; not observed in adults
* EPSC kinetics (rising and falling phases) were **significantly slower** in P11-P13 vs adults (*p*
  < 0.0001); no significant difference between first and second flash within an age group (*p* >
  0.05), indicating receptor composition differences rather than presynaptic depletion
* Synaptic reliability (R2/R1 ratio) was markedly reduced in early postnatal cells (*p* < 0.05)
* Dark-reared (P0-P11 or P0-P30) DSGCs showed DSI and tuning-curve width **identical** to normally
  reared controls (*p* > 0.05)
* Direction-selective responses first detectable at **P11**, the earliest age of any light-evoked
  spiking

## Innovations

### Dissociation of Excitability from Directional Computation

First systematic whole-cell characterisation of ON-OFF DSGCs at P11. The paper shows that DSI and
tuning width match adult values despite 2-fold lower spike counts and much slower EPSC kinetics,
establishing that directional computation is robust to large differences in excitability and
synaptic strength. For compartmental modelling, a model optimised to adult tuning should be tested
for robustness when excitability parameters are halved.

### Quantitative Tuning Curve Benchmark Across Four Developmental Stages

Angle-resolved response data at four time points (P11, P13, P18, adult) using a standardised
12-direction protocol at 30-degree resolution. This provides a benchmark dataset of DSI values and
tuning-curve half-widths for direct comparison with model output.

### Prolonged Dark-Rearing Experiment

P0-P30 dark-rearing demonstrates that a full month of visual deprivation leaves the
direction-selective circuit intact, establishing genetic pre-specification and ruling out
activity-dependent tuning mechanisms.

## Main Ideas

* The quantitative tuning data -- DSI values and half-widths from a standardised 12-direction
  protocol at 30-degree resolution -- provide the primary patch-clamp validation target for the
  compartmental model angle-to-AP-frequency tuning curve
* The dissociation between excitability and directional tuning implies circuit topology (input
  arrangement) is the dominant determinant of DSI; sensitivity analyses should test whether large
  changes in Na/K conductance magnitudes preserve tuning shape
* The specific stimulus parameters (bar 100 um x 500 um, approximately 750 um/s, 12 directions at
  30-degree intervals) should be replicated exactly in the model wave protocol for direct comparison
* EPSC kinetics are dominated by receptor subunit composition differences rather than presynaptic
  depletion, constraining how synaptic input kinetics are modelled
* The DSI > 0.3 criterion defines the minimum threshold that the model preferred-to-null response
  ratio should exceed

## Summary

Chen et al. (2009) investigate the electrophysiology of ON-OFF direction-selective retinal ganglion
cells across postnatal development in the C57BL/6N mouse, asking when directional computation first
appears and whether visual experience shapes its development. Using whole-cell patch clamp in the
isolated retina, they record from DSGCs at P11, P13, P18, and adulthood, and test mice reared in
complete darkness from P0-P11 and P0-P30.

The approach combines loose-patch identification (DSI > 0.3 threshold), whole-cell voltage-clamp for
EPSC kinetics at -65 mV and 0 mV, and current-clamp for excitability and directional tuning curves
(12 directions, 30-degree spacing, bar 100 um x 500 um at approximately 750 um/s). Cells were
morphologically confirmed as bistratified ON-OFF DSGCs by intracellular dye fills.

The central result is a developmental dissociation: spike counts are roughly half adult values at
P11 (ON: **21.2 +/- 4.4** vs adult **42.9 +/- 5.1 spikes**; peak rate **95.9** vs **166.4 Hz**),
EPSC kinetics are significantly slower (*p* < 0.0001), and synaptic reliability is markedly reduced
-- yet DSI and tuning-curve half-width are adult-equivalent at all ages (*p* > 0.05). Dark rearing
does not alter directional tuning, confirming the circuit is fully light-independent.

For this project, Chen et al. (2009) supply the primary empirical validation target. The
quantitative tuning metrics (DSI, half-width) from the standardised 12-direction protocol define the
target angle-to-AP-frequency relationship the compartmental model must reproduce. The specific
stimulus parameters should be replicated verbatim in the model wave protocol. The robustness of
directional tuning to excitability variations motivates sensitivity analyses in which somatic Na/K
conductances are varied widely without expecting the tuning curve to collapse.
